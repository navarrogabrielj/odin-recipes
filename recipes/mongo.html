Mongo

Mongo is a comforting Filipino mung bean stew made with mung beans, garlic, onions, and tomatoes, often enriched with slices of meat or seafood,such as pork or shrimp. The dish is typically seasoned with fish sauce and simmered until the beans are tender and the flavors meld together. It is commonly served with steamed rice, offering a hearty and nutritious meal.

Ingredients

  • 1 Â½ cup mung beans, or munggo
  • 1/2 lb (250 g) pork, thinly sliced
  • 1 pc medium-sized onion, chopped
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 pcs tomato, diced
  • 2 cups spinach, (or malunggay or alugbati leaves)
  • 3 tbsp patis, fish sauce
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 tbsp cooking oil
  • 10 pcs shrimp, peeled and deveined, optional
  • Chicharon (pork rind), optional topping
  • Tinapa (smoked fish), shredded, optional topping

Steps

  1. Clean the mung beans by discarding dirt and bad beans then wash with running water.
  2. Pour-in the water into a cooking pot and bring to a boil.
  3. Add-in the mung beans and simmer until soft and tender (about 30 to 45 minutes), then set aside. You may crush the mung beans with the back of the spoon.
  4. In another cooking pot, pour-in and heat the cooking oil.
  5. When hot enough, add-in the garlic, onion, and tomato and saute until tender and aromatic.
  6. Add-in the pork and cook for 5 minutes.
  7. Add-the fish sauce and simmer for 5 minutes to release the flavor of the pork (you may also add water and simmer for another 5 minutes to tenderize the pork).
  8. Add-in the shrimp and cook for 2 minutes.
  9. Add-in the cooked mung beans (including the water).
  10. Stir then simmer for 10 minutes.
  11. Add-in the spinach and cook for 1 more minute.
  12. Remove from heat and add optional toppings. Transfer to a serving bowl and enjoy!
